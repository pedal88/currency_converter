Exchange Rates for Dummies

What is an Exchange Rate? ü§î

An exchange rate is simply how much one currency is worth in terms of another currency. Think of it like trading baseball cards - just like you might trade two regular cards for one rare card, you trade one type of money for another at a specific rate.

Example with Pizza üçï

Let's say a pizza costs $10 in the USA. If the exchange rate is:

  • 1 USD = 0.92 EUR
  • 1 USD = 10.71 NOK

This means the same pizza would cost:

  • 107.10 NOK in Norway

Why Do Exchange Rates Change? üìà

Exchange rates change all the time, like prices in a store. They change because of things like:

  • How well a country's economy is doing
  • Political events and decisions
  • How much people want to buy or sell a currency
  • Interest rates in different countries

Tips for Understanding Exchange Rates üí°

  • Base Currency: This is your starting point (usually USD in our app)
  • Higher number doesn't mean stronger: NOK being 10.71 doesn't mean it's stronger than EUR at 0.92
  • Check daily: Rates change frequently, so always check current rates
  • Watch for fees: Banks and exchange services often charge extra fees
